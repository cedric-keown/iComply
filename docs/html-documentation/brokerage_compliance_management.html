The FSP Owner's Compliance Challenge

As a Financial Services Provider employing multiple brokers/representatives, you carry legal responsibility not just for your own compliance, but for every representative operating under your FSP license.

Your Legal Liability as FSP Principal

FAIS Act Section 13(2)(a): "An authorised financial services provider is responsible for any act or omission by its representatives when rendering financial services."

  • Vicarious Liability: You are legally liable for your representatives' actions, even if you weren't directly involved
  • License at Risk: One non-compliant representative can trigger FSCA action against your entire FSP license
  • Financial Exposure: R10 million maximum fine applies to the FSP entity
  • Reputational Damage: Non-compliance by any representative affects your entire brokerage
  • Personal Liability: Key Individuals can face personal debarment for oversight failures

Bottom Line: You MUST have real-time visibility and control over every representative's compliance status.

Your Compliance Obligations as an FSP

1. Representative Management & Supervision

FAIS Act Section 13 & 17 Requirements

You must ensure ALL representatives meet and maintain:

  • Registration: Every representative must be registered with FSCA under your FSP
  • Fit & Proper Compliance: Continuous monitoring of qualifications, exams, training
  • Supervision: Adequate oversight and supervision of all representatives
  • Competence Verification: Regular assessment of competence to render advice
  • Code of Conduct: All representatives must sign and adhere to your code
What You Need to Track:
  • Each representative's registration status
  • RE1/RE5 certificate expiry dates
  • Class of Business training validity
  • Qualifications and credentials
  • CPD hours progress (per person)
  • Criminal clearance certificates
  • Credit status checks
  • Debarment status checks
Platform Solution:

Team Compliance Dashboard - Real-time view of every representative's compliance status with automated alerts for expiring credentials.

2. Team CPD Management

FAIS Act Section 8A - Continuous Professional Development

As FSP owner, you're responsible for ensuring EVERY representative completes their required CPD annually:

  • Variable Hours: 6-20 hours depending on each person's authorisations
  • Mandatory Ethics: Minimum 3 hours ethics per person per year
  • Deadline: 31 May annually - missing this = non-compliance
  • Verification: Must retain proof from accredited providers
  • Consequences: Non-compliant representatives cannot render financial services
The Challenge Without Automation:
  • Tracking 10+ representatives manually = spreadsheet nightmare
  • Different CPD requirements per person based on their authorisations
  • Chasing representatives for certificates
  • Verifying accredited providers
  • Risk of missing 31 May deadline for entire team
Platform Solution:

CPD Tracking Dashboard - Automatic calculation of required hours per person, progress tracking, automated reminders, and team compliance overview.

3. Compliance Officer Function

FAIS Act Section 17 Requirements

You MUST appoint a Compliance Officer who:

  • Meets Fit & Proper: Specific qualifications and experience required
  • Independent Function: Must be able to operate independently
  • Reporting: Regular reports to FSCA on compliance status
  • Monitoring: Ongoing monitoring of FSP and representatives
  • Risk Management: Identify and mitigate compliance risks
What the Compliance Officer Needs:
  • Complete visibility of all representatives
  • Automated compliance monitoring
  • Exception reporting
  • Audit trail of all activities
  • Risk assessment tools
  • Report generation for FSCA
  • Document review workflows
  • Remediation tracking
Platform Solution:

Compliance Officer Portal - Dedicated tools for monitoring, reporting, and managing compliance across the entire organization.

4. FICA Compliance Management

FICA Act - FSPs as Accountable Institutions

Your brokerage is an Accountable Institution and must:

  • Risk Management Program: Board-approved FICA compliance program
  • Client Verification: KYC on ALL clients across all representatives
  • Ongoing Monitoring: Regular reviews of client risk profiles
  • Training: All representatives must be FICA-trained
  • Reporting: Suspicious transaction reports to FIC
  • Record Keeping: 5-year retention of all FICA documentation
The Multi-Representative Challenge:
  • Each representative deals with different clients
  • Inconsistent verification processes
  • Fragmented documentation across the team
  • No centralized view of high-risk clients
  • Review deadlines missed for different clients
  • Audit trail gaps
Platform Solution:

Centralized FICA Management - Single client database across all representatives, standardized verification workflows, automated review scheduling, and risk dashboards.

5. Document Management & Record Keeping

FAIS Act Section 17 & 19 Requirements

You must maintain comprehensive records for 5 years:

  • All Client Interactions: Advice records, ROAs, mandates, correspondence
  • Transaction Records: Applications, policies, amendments, claims
  • Representative Records: Qualifications, CPD, training, performance
  • Compliance Records: Internal audits, FSCA correspondence, reports
  • Complaints: Complete complaints file with resolutions
  • FICA Documentation: All KYC and verification records
Scale Problem:

With 10 representatives each handling 100 clients = 1,000 client files to manage. Each file needs:

  • Proper categorization and indexing
  • 5-year retention tracking
  • Quick retrieval for FSCA audits
  • Version control
  • Secure storage
  • Access controls
Platform Solution:

Enterprise Document Management - Centralized repository with automatic retention, advanced search, role-based access, and complete audit trails.

6. Complaints Management

FAIS Act Section 26 & General Code of Conduct

You must have a complaints resolution system that:

  • Internal Process: Clear escalation and resolution procedures
  • Timeframes: 6 weeks for resolution, 6 months maximum
  • Ombudsman: Advise clients of right to escalate
  • Records: Maintain complete complaint files for 5 years
  • Reporting: Regular complaints reporting to FSCA
  • Analysis: Identify trends and systemic issues
Platform Solution:

Complaints Workflow Management - Track complaints, monitor resolution times, generate reports, and identify training needs.

7. Treating Customers Fairly (TCF)

FSCA TCF Requirements (Conduct Standard)

You must demonstrate that your business:

  • Fair Outcomes: Clients receive fair outcomes throughout product lifecycle
  • Monitoring: Measure and monitor TCF outcomes
  • Reporting: Regular TCF reports to FSCA
  • Culture: TCF embedded in business culture and remuneration
  • Product Design: Products meet client needs
  • Disclosure: Clear, fair communications
