The Compliance Challenge

The Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) enforces strict compliance requirements under the Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services (FAIS) Act 37 of 2002. Non-compliance can result in license suspension, heavy fines, criminal prosecution, and reputational damage.

Critical 2014 Legislative Amendments

FAIS Circular 4 of 2014 implemented significant amendments via the Financial Services Laws General Amendment Act (FSLGA) 45 of 2013, effective 28 February 2014:

  • Section 6A Inserted: Fit & Proper Requirements now statutory law
  • Section 8A Inserted: CPD became mandatory fit and proper requirement
  • Section 17 Amended: Stricter compliance officer requirements
  • Section 36 Amended: Maximum fine increased 1000% from R1M to R10M
  • Section 38A-C Inserted: Business rescue, liquidation, and directive powers
  • Enforcement Strengthened: FSCA given broader powers and tougher sanctions

What This Means: Compliance is no longer a "best practice" - it's statutory law with severe criminal and financial penalties. The R10M maximum fine demonstrates FSCA's commitment to strict enforcement.

Legislative Mandate - Not Optional

FAIS Act 37 of 2002 (as amended by Financial Services Laws General Amendment Act 45 of 2013) establishes statutory obligations for all Financial Services Providers (FSPs), Key Individuals (KIs), and Representatives:

  • Section 6A: Fit and Proper Requirements (inserted 2014)
  • Section 8A: Continuous Professional Development - mandatory legal requirement (inserted 2014)
  • Section 13: Representatives must be competent and meet COB training
  • Section 17: Compliance Officers appointed and reporting requirements
  • FICA Act 38 of 2001: FSPs are Accountable Institutions

Source: FAIS Act 37 of 2002, FSLGA Act 45 of 2013, FAIS Circular 4 of 2014, Board Notice 194 of 2017

Legal Consequences of Non-Compliance

FAIS Act Section 36 - Penalties (as amended 2014):

  • License Withdrawal: FSCA can suspend or withdraw your FSP license (Section 9)
  • Financial Penalties: Maximum fine increased to R10,000,000 per violation (amended from R1M in 2014)
  • Criminal Prosecution: Imprisonment for up to 10 years (Section 36)
  • Representative Debarment: Permanent ban from the financial services industry (Section 14A)
  • Reputational Damage: Public disclosure on FSCA website, loss of client trust
  • Administrative Sanctions: Additional penalties under Financial Institutions Act

Critical Update: The 2014 amendments increased maximum fines from R1 million to R10 million - a 1000% increase demonstrating FSCA's serious enforcement posture.

Source: FAIS Act Section 36 (as amended by FSLGA 2013), FAIS Circular 4 of 2014, FSCA Enforcement Actions

1. Fit & Proper Management

Legislative Requirement: FAIS Act Section 6A (Inserted 2014)

Section 6A of the FAIS Act establishes the legal obligation for all FSPs, Key Individuals, and Representatives to meet and maintain Fit and Proper requirements:

  • Honesty & Integrity (Section 6A(2)(a)): Good standing with no criminal record, debarments, or adverse credit history
  • Competency (Section 6A(2)(b)): Approved qualifications, RE1 and RE5 exams (minimum 65%), and Class of Business training
  • Operational Ability (Section 6A(2)(c)): Adequate systems, governance, and risk management
  • Financial Soundness (Section 6A(2)(d)): Demonstrated financial stability
  • Continuous Compliance (Section 8A): Ongoing adherence to requirements, not just at authorization

Board Notice 194 of 2017 provides detailed Fit and Proper requirements including:

  • Minimum experience for Key Individuals (typically 3-5 years)
  • Specific qualification requirements per product category
  • Class of Business training requirements
  • Ongoing monitoring and reporting obligations

Source: FAIS Act Section 6A & 8A (2014), Section 13, Board Notice 194 of 2017

Why You Need This Module:

Legal Risk: Representatives operating without valid qualifications or COB training violate Section 13 and can result in immediate license suspension (Section 9), personal debarment (Section 14A), and fines up to R10 million (Section 36).

2. CPD Management & Tracking

Legislative Requirement: FAIS Act Section 8A (Inserted 2014)

Section 8A makes Continuous Professional Development a mandatory fit and proper requirement - not a guideline, but law. Failure to complete CPD renders a representative non-compliant with Section 8A.

Board Notice 194 of 2017 specifies CPD requirements based on authorisations:

  • 6-12 hours: Single subclass or single class of business
  • 18 hours: Multiple classes of business (most common for brokers)
  • 20 hours: Long-term insurance, health insurance, or investments
  • Minimum 3 hours: Ethics and Practice Standards (mandatory component)
  • CPD Cycle: 1 June to 31 May annually
  • Accreditation: Must be from FSCA-recognized professional bodies (FPI, IOBSA, SAIFM)
  • Verification: CPD certificates must be retained as proof of compliance

Legal Consequence: Section 8A non-compliance means the representative does not meet fit and proper requirements, triggering potential license suspension under Section 9.

Source: FAIS Act Section 8A (2014), Section 13, Board Notice 194 of 2017 - Determination of CPD

Why You Need This Module:

  • Ethics Tracking: Separate tracking ensures minimum 3 ethics hours are met
  • Team Oversight: Key Individuals can monitor entire team's compliance status
  • Pro-Rata Calculations: Automatic adjustments for leave, new appointments
Legal Risk: Non-compliance with CPD requirements (Section 8A) leads to immediate suspension of representatives and potential debarment (Section 14A). FSCA conducts regular audits of CPD records. Maximum penalty: R10 million fine and/or 10 years imprisonment (Section 36).

3. FICA Client Verification Workflow

Regulatory Requirement (FICA Act 38 of 2001)

All FSPs are Accountable Institutions under FICA and must:

  • Verify Client Identity: Obtain and verify ID document and proof of residence (< 3 months)
  • Risk Assessment: Classify clients as Low (SDD), Medium (CDD), or High Risk (EDD)
  • PEP Screening: Screen all clients against Politically Exposed Person databases
  • Sanctions Screening: Check against OFAC, UN, EU, HMT sanctions lists
  • Ongoing Monitoring: Regular reviews (annual for CDD, quarterly for EDD)
  • Record Keeping: Maintain all FICA records for 5 years
  • Suspicious Activity Reporting: Report STRs/SARs to Financial Intelligence Centre

Source: Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001, FIC Guidance Notes

Why You Need This Module:
  • Step-by-Step Workflow: Guided 6-step verification process ensures nothing is missed
  • Automated Screening: Integrated PEP and sanctions screening (simulated)
  • Risk Calculator: Automatic risk assessment based on FICA guidelines
  • Document Management: Centralized storage of all FICA documentation
  • Review Scheduler: Automatic reminders for annual/quarterly reviews
  • Audit Ready: Complete audit trail for FIC inspections
  • Compliance Dashboard: Real-time visibility of verification status across all clients
Legal Risk: FICA violations can result in administrative sanctions up to R100 million (FICA Act), criminal prosecution under FICA, and FAIS Act penalties up to R10 million (Section 36). South Africa was grey-listed by FATF until 2024, significantly increasing regulatory scrutiny of AML/CFT compliance.

4. Document Management & 5-Year Retention

Regulatory Requirement (FAIS Act Section 17, FICA Act)

FSPs must maintain comprehensive records for a minimum of 5 years:

  • Transaction Records: All client transactions, advice given, and products recommended
  • Client Documentation: Agreements, risk profiles, FICA documents
  • Correspondence: All communications with clients
  • Compliance Records: CPD certificates, qualifications, internal audits
  • Complaints: Complete records of all complaints and resolutions
  • Calculation: Retention starts from transaction/document date, not upload date
  • Format: Must be readily accessible and retrievable

Source: FAIS Act Section 17, FICA Act Sections 22-23

Why You Need This Module:
  • Automated Retention: System calculates 5-year retention period from document date
  • Centralized Storage: Single secure repository for all compliance documents
  • Smart Categorization: Automatic organization by type, client, representative
  • Expiry Alerts: Notifications 90, 60, 30 days before documents require renewal
  • Version Control: Track all document versions with complete history
  • Audit Trail: Every document access, modification, and deletion is logged
  • Legal Hold: Prevent deletion of documents under dispute or investigation
  • Search & Retrieve: Instantly find any document for FSCA inspections
Legal Risk: Inability to produce required documents during FSCA audit (Section 4 inspection powers) can result in immediate license suspension (Section 9), substantial fines up to R10 million (Section 36), and criminal prosecution. Manual record-keeping significantly increases risk of lost, expired, or non-compliant documents.
